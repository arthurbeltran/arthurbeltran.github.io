The median legislator might propose his or her ideal point, but will only do so if that policy will have sufficient support. Otherwise, the median legislator may need to propose a policy closer to the status quo than his or her ideal point in order to prevent a filibuster. For instance, if the status quo is $7.30, a proposal of $10 (the median ideal point) would be defeated. Consequently, the median legislator might propose a policy closer to the preferences of Cameron (say $8.50) in order to win her support and prevent / end a filibuster. In this way filibusters often spur negotiation and compromise.
